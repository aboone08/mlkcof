About

Established in 1984, the MLK Commission of Florida, Inc. focuses its leadership and resources on the provision of extensive training and educational services involving the interaction of groups involved in social and economic justice.

Mission

To continue the legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. by advocating and promoting nonviolently, the elimination of poverty and racism in Alachua County and the State of Florida, through education and research including collecting and preserving the full history of the African American culture.

Noteworthy Accomplishments

  • Over $88,500.00 in scholarships given to high school seniors
  • Created an $8,000.00 Educational Edowment with Sante Fe Community College for burned out residents of the Kennedy Homes Apartment Complex
  • Created a partnership with the University of Florida Office of the President to provide Thanksgiving and Christmas Baskets to needy residents of Alachua County
  • Provided community workshops on diversity, restoration of civil rights and other important issues in the community
  • Highway 441 was re-named the Martin Luther King, Jr., Memorial Highway by the Florida Legislature, reaching more than 30 miles through Alachua County from Columbia County to Marion County
  • Completed an Obelisk (memorial) in honor of Dr. King which stands in front of Gainesville City Hall in the Martin Luther King, Jr., Memorial Gardens
  • Co-sponsor of the Strike Out Hunger Food Drive in Alachua County
